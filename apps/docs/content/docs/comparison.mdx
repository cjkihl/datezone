> <p className="text-orange-200">Comparison is the thief of joy.</p>

Here's why you may want to use Datezone over other date and time libraries:

### vs date-fns
- **Smaller bundle size** (tree-shakeable, no locale bloat)
- **Native timezone support** (IANA, UTC, local)
- **Consistent, strict TypeScript types**
- **Performance-focused**
- **No dependencies**

### vs Luxon
- **Much smaller bundle size**
- **Faster operations**
- **Simpler API**
- **No dependency on Intl or polyfills**

### vs Day.js
- **Native timezone support**
- **Strict types**
- **No plugin bloat**
- **Better DST handling**

### vs Moment.js
- **Modern API**
- **Immutable by default**
- **Tiny bundle size**
- **Much faster**
- **Full timezone support**

### vs Rolling Your Own
- **Handles all edge cases** (DST, leap years, etc.)
- **Battle-tested**
- **Focus on your product, not date bugs**
